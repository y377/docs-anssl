The dashboard gives you a quick view of the anssl console. It aggregates certificate, domain monitor, and deployment client data, then shows health, state distribution, warnings, certificate request trends, and CA distribution.

## Open the dashboard

After signing in, you land on the [Dashboard](https://anssl.cn/console/dashboard) by default. You can also open **Workspace → Dashboard** from the console sidebar.

## Top actions

The page header includes:

- **Refresh**: refresh certificate, monitor, and deployment client data together.
- **Request certificate**: open [Certificate management](./certificate-management).
- **Dynamic summary**: a short generated summary such as healthy certificate count, certificates needing attention, renewals in progress, or abnormal monitors.

When there are no certificates, monitors, or deployment clients, the summary prompts you to request the first certificate.

## Warning banner

The dashboard shows a yellow warning banner when:

- Certificates need attention.
- Monitors have failed, expired, or are unreachable.

The banner includes shortcuts to Certificate management or Domain monitoring.

<Aside type="tip">
  When checking the dashboard daily, start with the warning banner. If no banner is shown and the summary says everything is normal, the core assets have no obvious issues.
</Aside>

## Health cards

The dashboard has three clickable health cards.

### Certificates

The certificate card shows:

- Total certificates.
- Health: healthy certificates / total certificates.
- State distribution: healthy, applying, failed, expired.

Click the card to open Certificate management.

### Domain monitoring

The monitor card shows:

- Total monitors.
- Health: healthy monitors / total monitors.
- State distribution: healthy, abnormal, unreachable, expired.

Click the card to open Domain monitoring.

### Automated deployment

The deployment card shows:

- Total deployment clients.
- Health: online clients / total clients.
- State distribution: online, offline, enabled, disabled.

Click the card to open Automated deployment.

<Aside type="note">
  A 0% health value does not always mean failure. If no assets are configured for that area, such as no deployment clients, health is also shown as 0%.
</Aside>

## Certificate request trend

The trend chart counts certificate requests by certificate creation time. You can switch the time range:

- 7 days.
- 30 days.
- 90 days.

Use it to spot concentrated certificate requests or recent growth that may need deployment and monitoring follow-up.

## CA distribution

The CA distribution chart groups current certificates by issuing CA:

- Google Trust Services.
- Let's Encrypt.
- ZeroSSL.
- SSL.com.
- Actalis.

If there are no certificates, the chart area shows an empty state.

## Suggested workflow

<Steps>

1. **Check the warning banner**

   If certificates need attention or monitors are abnormal, use the shortcut buttons first.

2. **Review the three health cards**

   Identify whether the weak area is certificates, monitors, or deployment clients.

3. **Check the trend**

   If many certificates were added recently, confirm they are deployed and monitored.

4. **Refresh after remediation**

   After fixing issues, return to the dashboard and refresh to confirm the summary and health values changed.

</Steps>

## FAQ

### Why is the trend empty?

The chart uses certificate creation time. If no certificates were created in the selected range, it shows an empty state. Try switching to 30 or 90 days.

### Why is deployment health low?

Deployment health is based on online clients. Check whether clients are offline, services stopped, networks blocked, or no deployment clients have been connected yet.

### Why does dashboard data differ from a list page?

Click **Refresh** on the dashboard. If it still differs, refresh the corresponding list page and check whether filters are applied there.
